<p><p>The medical-grade zirconia ceramics market is dominated by three main types: monoclinic, tetragonal, and cubic zirconia. Monoclinic zirconia, typically stable at room temperature, is often used in applications requiring high toughness. Tetragonal zirconia, transformed through heat treatment, offers improved strength and is widely used in dental and orthopedic implants. Cubic zirconia, known for its aesthetic qualities and translucency, is primarily utilized in dental prosthetics. Each type serves specific medical applications, contributing to advancements in biocompatibility and durability.</p></p>
