<p><p>Low Iron Clear Glass is a high-transparency glass with reduced iron content, offering minimal greenish tint and enhanced clarity. In the market, it is available in two primary types: Rolled Glass and Float Glass. Rolled Glass is manufactured by passing molten glass through rollers, creating a textured or patterned surface, often used in decorative applications. Float Glass, produced by floating molten glass on molten tin, results in a smooth, flat surface, ideal for architectural and high-clarity uses like windows and displays. Both types cater to distinct aesthetic and functional needs.</p></p>
