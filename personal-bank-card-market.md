<p><p>The personal bank card market encompasses several types, including charge cards, debit cards, credit cards, and payment cards. Charge cards require full payment of the outstanding balance each month, encouraging responsible spending. Debit cards directly withdraw funds from a linked bank account, providing real-time access to available cash. Credit cards allow users to borrow funds up to a limit, with the option to pay over time, usually with interest. Payment cards serve as a broad category that includes both debit and credit options for efficient transactions.</p></p>
