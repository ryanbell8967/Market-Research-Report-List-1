<p><ul><li>Ester process</li><li>Acid process</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Polyethylene Naphthalate (PEN) is a high-performance polymer produced via two primary methods: the Ester process and the Acid process. The Ester process involves esterification of naphthalene dicarboxylic acid with ethylene glycol, offering high purity and efficiency, making it suitable for advanced applications like electronics and packaging. The Acid process uses direct polymerization of naphthalene dicarboxylic acid with ethylene glycol, often resulting in cost-effective production, ideal for industrial and bulk applications. Both methods cater to diverse market needs, balancing quality and cost.</p></p>
