<p><p>The Transient Voltage Suppressor (TVS) diode market is primarily segmented into uni-polar and bi-polar types. Uni-polar TVS diodes are designed to clamp voltage spikes in a single polarity, making them ideal for DC applications where negative voltage transients are absent. In contrast, bi-polar TVS diodes can handle voltage transients in both polarities, offering protection in AC applications and circuits that experience fluctuations in both positive and negative voltage. Both types serve critical roles in safeguarding electronic components from transient overvoltage conditions.</p></p>
