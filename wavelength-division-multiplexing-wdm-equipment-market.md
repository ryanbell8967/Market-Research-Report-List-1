<p><p>The Wavelength Division Multiplexing (WDM) equipment market is categorized into two primary types: Coarse Wavelength Division Multiplexing (CWDM) and Dense Wavelength Division Multiplexing (DWDM). CWDM uses a broader wavelength spacing, allowing for lower-cost solutions ideal for shorter distances, making it suitable for metropolitan networks. In contrast, DWDM features tighter wavelength spacing, enabling a higher number of channels over longer distances, ideal for long-haul communication and high-capacity networks. Both technologies enhance bandwidth efficiency in optical fiber communications.</p></p>
